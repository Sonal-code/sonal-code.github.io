Where You Are Now

The Honest Starting Point

Most artists who reach $15,000+ started exactly here. The gap isn't about talent — it's about positioning, market access, and building the right career infrastructure. Here's the reality of where you are versus where you're going.

Today
—
  • 📍 Selling via online & direct channels
  • 🎨 Original works
  • 👥 Small collector base, no waitlist
  • 📱 Building social presence
  • 🏛️ No gallery representation yet
  • 💰 Inconsistent, low revenue per sale
Target: Year 5
—
  • 🏛️ Represented by a respected gallery
  • 🌊 Known for a distinctive, recognisable style
  • 📋 Collector waitlist for new works
  • 🏆 Art prize wins on record
  • 📰 Press features — editorial coverage
  • 💼 Corporate & hotel commissions

The real reason paintings sell for $15,000 is not because they're 30× better than a $500 painting. It's because the artist has built a track record, a narrative, gallery credibility, and collector demand. Your job over the next 5 years is to build those four things systematically — while making the best work of your life.

The most important rule: Never discount. If a painting isn't selling at your current price, the answer is almost never to lower the price — it's to find a different audience, improve the presentation, or create a series narrative. Discounting destroys collector confidence in your future prices.

The Strategy

Three Phases to $15K

Each phase has a clear focus. Don't try to do everything at once. Phase 1 is about building foundations. Phase 2 is about gaining credibility. Phase 3 is about leveraging it.

Phase 1 — Years 1 to 2
Build Your Foundation & Identity
Develop a signature style, create a body of work, start exhibiting
Target price
$800–$2,500
🌊 Define Your Two Series
Commit to two distinct bodies of work: Coastal Abstracts (beaches, water, light) and City Street Scenes (Melbourne laneways, Sydney streets). Name each series. A collector buying "Part 3 of the Coast Series" feels they own something significant.
→ Commands 2× price of a standalone painting
📐 Go Larger
Move from small/medium to large format — 90×120cm minimum for key pieces. Large works command higher prices, photograph better, and look more impressive in galleries and homes. One large work beats three small ones for career building.
→ Immediately justifies $1,500–$2,500 per piece
✍️ Write Your Artist Statement
One page. Who you are, what drives you, what your work is about. Be specific — not "I am inspired by nature" but "I paint the moment light hits Bondi water at 6am — that tension between stillness and energy." This story is what galleries and collectors buy.
→ Required for every gallery application
📸 Professional Photography
Every painting must be photographed properly — white wall, natural light, no reflections, high resolution. This is non-negotiable. A $1,500 painting photographed badly looks like a $200 painting. Spend $300–500 on a shoot covering 20+ works.
→ Transforms online and gallery presence
🏆 Enter Art Prizes
Research and enter every relevant Australian prize: Paddington Art Prize, Fisher's Ghost Art Award, Waverley Art Prize, Blake Prize, Royal Easter Show Art competition. You won't win immediately — but being entered builds your CV and being selected is a major credit.
→ Each selection = legitimate CV entry
🖼️ First Group Exhibitions
Apply to local gallery open calls, art society exhibitions, and pop-up group shows. Start with boutique galleries in your city. The goal isn't to sell — it's to have exhibition credits on your CV and to meet gallerists. Introduce yourself after the show.
→ First gallery credits open doors
Phase 2 — Years 2 to 4
Build Credibility & Gallery Access
Gallery representation, media coverage, collector relationships
Target price
$4,000–$10,000
🏛️ Pursue Gallery Representation
Target boutique galleries in Melbourne and Sydney that represent emerging abstract artists. Send a professional approach package: CV, artist statement, 10 high-res images, and a cover letter specific to that gallery. Follow up once. Don't mass-email.
→ Gallery = instant price credibility
🎪 Solo Exhibition
Your first solo show is a turning point. Even a small gallery or café gallery space counts. Create 15–20 new works specifically for the show, write an exhibition statement, host an opening night. Every solo show is a permanent CV entry.
→ Solo show = artist, not hobbyist
📰 Pitch for Press
Contact local newspapers, lifestyle magazines, and arts blogs. The angle: "local artist capturing [your city's] soul through abstract painting." Journalists need content — give them a story hook. A single editorial feature can bring 10× more collectors than months of social media.
→ Press builds collector trust
🏨 Target Interior Designers & Hotels
Corporate and hospitality art is where $3,000–$15,000 sales happen regularly. Build a portfolio specifically targeting interior designers — coastal works for coastal properties, urban works for city hotels. Make an outreach list of 20 designers and approach them personally.
→ One hotel can buy 6–10 works at once
🎨 Limited Edition Prints
Release limited edition archival prints of your best originals — editions of 20–50, signed and numbered, at $300–$800 each. This funds your practice, introduces new collectors at lower price points, and drives demand for the originals. Use a high-quality print-on-demand service.
→ Passive income while originals grow in value
📋 Build Your Collector List
Every person who buys, inquires, or attends your opening goes on a personal list. Email them personally when you have new work before anyone else sees it. Collectors who buy twice become long-term patrons. Give them early access, behind-the-scenes updates, studio visits.
→ Repeat collectors = career stability
Phase 3 — Years 4 to 5
Leverage & Scale
Waitlist, auctions, national recognition, $15K+ prices
Target price
$12,000–$20,000
🔒 Create Scarcity
Announce that you're producing only 12–15 original large works per year. Have your gallery manage a waitlist. Scarcity is the engine of high prices. When collectors know availability is limited and prices are rising, they buy earlier and pay more.
→ Waitlist = $15K becomes expected
🏺 Enter the Auction Market
Work with your gallery to have a painting entered into a reputable auction — Mossgreen, Deutscher & Hackett, or Bonhams. Even a $4,000–$8,000 auction result establishes a public price record, which supports your gallery prices significantly.
→ Auction record = price benchmark
🌏 Art Fairs
Sydney Contemporary and Melbourne Art Fair are where serious collectors shop. Exhibit through your gallery — this is exposure to hundreds of collectors, curators, and press in a single weekend. International art fairs come next.
→ One fair can cover a year of sales
🏛️ Museum / Public Collection
Actively seek to have a work acquired by a regional gallery, university collection, or corporate art collection. Even a small institution counts. "In the collection of..." on your CV is worth more than almost anything else for justifying high prices.
→ Institutional recognition = career legitimacy

Gallery Strategy

The Gallery Pathway

Gallery representation is the single biggest lever for reaching $15,000 prices. Galleries bring collectors, credibility, and price authority. Here's how to get there in the Australian market.

Now — Year 1
Open Calls & Group Shows
Enter every gallery open call, art society exhibition, and community group show you can find. These build your exhibition CV and get your work in front of gallerists. Don't wait to be "ready."
Look for: Bluethumb exhibitions, local arts council shows, café galleries, open studio events
Year 1–2
Boutique & Regional Galleries
Approach smaller commercial galleries — they're more open to emerging artists than major galleries. Research which galleries show abstract work in your style. Send a professional approach package. Be patient — it may take 6–12 months to get a response.
Targets: regional NSW/VIC galleries, boutique Melbourne & Sydney spaces, coastal tourist galleries (who sell coastal art every day)
Year 2–3
First Gallery Representation
Your goal is to be represented by at least one commercial gallery — meaning they hold your work, promote it to their clients, and take 40–50% commission on sales. That commission is worth it: they bring the collectors to you, handle the selling, and legitimise your prices.
What galleries want to see: 10+ exhibition credits, consistent body of work, professional CV, reliable output, collector interest already demonstrated
Year 4–5
Established Gallery Relationship
By now you have 1–2 galleries representing you, regular solo shows, and a collector base that follows your work across exhibitions. Your gallery sets your prices — and they'll confidently put $12,000–$20,000 on works when they know collectors will buy.
Aspire to: Nanda\Hobbs, THIS IS NO FANTASY, Heiser Gallery, Niagara Galleries, Art Collective WA

Coastal art is a powerful niche in Australia. Gallery owners in coastal regions — Noosa, Mornington Peninsula, Byron Bay, Port Douglas — sell coastal abstract art to holiday homeowners and sea-change buyers constantly. These galleries are often more accessible than city galleries and their clientele spend freely. Target these first.

Gallery Application

What Galleries Actually Look For

Most galleries receive hundreds of applications. Here's exactly what separates the artists they take on from the ones they politely decline.

✅
A Consistent, Recognisable Body of WorkNot a mix of everything you've ever tried. A clear series of 15–20 works that hang together and tell a story. Gallery owners need to picture your work on their walls and know it fits their collectors.
✅
Professional CV with Exhibition HistoryAt least 5–10 exhibition credits, any prizes entered or won, any press mentions, any public or corporate collections. The CV tells them you're serious and not going to disappear.
✅
A Strong Artist StatementOne page. Specific about your influences, process, and what your work is about. Not vague. A gallerist needs to be able to explain your work to a collector in 30 seconds.
✅
Professional Images of Your WorkHigh resolution, properly lit, on clean white or neutral wall. This is often the first thing they see. If it looks amateurish, everything else is moot.
✅
Evidence of Market InterestIf you've already sold work — even at $200–$500 — that shows your work connects with buyers. Mention it. "I have sold work through Bluethumb and private commissions and have a small but growing collector base" is a positive signal.
✅
You've Done Your ResearchYour cover letter references specific artists they represent and explains why your work is a natural fit for their gallery. Generic applications go straight to the bottom. Show you know their programme.
Brand & Audience

Building Your Artist Profile Online

Your online presence isn't separate from your art career — it's part of it. Collectors research artists before they buy. Galleries look at your following. Press checks your Instagram. Here's what to build and how.

📸
Instagram — Your Primary Platform
Post process videos (time-lapses of you painting), finished works, studio life, and exhibition openings. Coastal art performs exceptionally well. Target: 3 posts/week, focus on reels. Use location tags for your coastal subjects.
🌐
Artist Website
A clean, professional portfolio site with your CV, artist statement, and gallery of available works. This is where galleries and press go. Simple is better — let the paintings be the hero. Include a "collector enquiry" contact form.
📧
Collector Email List
More valuable than any social platform. Every buyer, inquirer, and opening-night visitor goes on this list. Monthly email: new work, upcoming exhibitions, behind-the-scenes. Collectors buy from artists they feel connected to.
🎬
Process Videos
Short videos of your painting process are irresistible content — especially abstract work where the transformation is dramatic. These consistently go viral in art communities and attract both collectors and press attention.
🖼️
Bluethumb — Evolving Role
Keep your Bluethumb presence but evolve it. Use it for prints and lower-priced original works while your gallery represents your flagship pieces. Don't abandon it — it introduces new collectors who may graduate to gallery prices.
📰
PR & Press Strategy
Write a media release for each exhibition and send to local lifestyle editors. The hook: an Australian woman building a serious art career from scratch — that's genuinely interesting. Local stories lead to national ones.

The One Thing That Changes Everything

Every artist who reaches $15,000 prices has one thing in common: they committed to being an artist, not a hobbyist who sells paintings. That means treating it like a business — tracking your CV, applying consistently to prizes and galleries, investing in professional photography, building collector relationships, and never stopping when a gallery says no.

Your subject matter is genuinely commercial in the Australian market. Coastal abstracts and city street scenes are two of the most consistently bought categories — by interior designers, by homeowners in sea-change suburbs, by hotels and restaurants, by corporate collectors. The market is there. The question is whether you're positioned to access it.

Start now. Apply to something this week.
